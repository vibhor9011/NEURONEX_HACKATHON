Rehabilitation Engineering Logic

Traditional stroke-rehabilitation BCIs use digital signal processing (DSP) (like Fourier transforms or filter banks) over windowed data (100–300ms). This introduces severe processing latency and consumes substantial power (running heavy matrix multiplications constantly on digital microcontrollers).

Our neuromorphic system mirrors the biological brain:

  • Delta Modulation (Sensory Input): Converts continuous voltages (EEG/EMG) into asynchronous spike events. Spikes are only generated when there is a significant change in signal amplitude ($\pm \theta$). When the patient is idle, no spikes are generated, resulting in near-zero idle power.
  • Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF) SNN Core: Receives spike trains asynchronously. Incoming spikes depolarize the membrane potentials ($V_m$) of specific detector neurons. If the user performs motor imagery, the reduction in Mu-band EEG spikes and the corresponding increase in EMG activity cause the Grasp LIF neuron to integrate these impulses and cross its threshold, firing an output trigger.
  • Ultra-low Latency: Decision triggers fire the instant the membrane threshold is crossed. This happens within milliseconds of voluntary intent, staying well within the biological sensorimotor loop requirement ($<25$ ms), which promotes neuroplastic recovery.

How to Interpret SANI Visual Data

A simple guide to understanding the neural charts and stats

EEG (Mu/Beta Wave)

Represents motor planning signals in the brain cortex. At rest, it displays continuous 10Hz waves (Mu rhythm). When the patient tries to move, this rhythm collapses (desynchronizes) and flattens out, indicating volitional motor imagery.

EMG (Forearm Contraction)

Represents electrical activity generated by forearm muscle fibers. Voluntary movement attempts show up as spiky, high-frequency voltage bursts. Relaxed states show a flat line with minimal background noise.

UP/DOWN Spike Events

Asynchronous events generated when analog voltages deviate up (+) or down (-) by more than the delta threshold (θ). Spikes represent sensory action potentials. No signal change = no spikes = zero active processing power.

LIF Membrane Potentials

Represents the temporary electrical charge accumulation ($V_m$) in the detector neurons. Muscle sensory spikes add positive charge. Brain resting EEG spikes add negative (inhibitory) charge. Firing occurs once the potential climbs past the dashed threshold line.

Neuromorphic Latency

The time (in milliseconds) from the initial motor attempt to the exoskeleton firing. Neuromorphic SNNs process spikes immediately as they arrive, yielding latency <10ms, which fits within the organic sensorimotor loop (<25ms) to stimulate neuroplastic recovery.

Spasm Overrides

Uncontrolled spastic muscle firing generates high-frequency EMG spikes in both muscle groups. The SNN Spasm neuron detects this co-contraction pattern immediately and locks the exoskeleton actuators, displaying a warning message to prevent injury.
